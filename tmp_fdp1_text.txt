Abstract
The operation of the HU non-Boarding Secondary School depends heavily on the
managing of student academic and administrative information. The current student
record keeping system is based on manual and paper-based system at HU Non-
Boarding Secondary School that has in turn created challenges with delays in
registering students and preparing student reports, data inconsistency, record loss
risk, and high workload. With the increasing number of the students, the current
system has become ineffective and hard to sustain. Considering this problem this
project aims with designing of a web-based Student Information Management System
to streamline the manner in which student information is stored, accessed, processed
and collected. The proposed system will automate the major school activities such as
student registration, attendance tracking, academic record management, schedule
arrangement, report and transcript creation and communication between
administrators, teachers, students and parents. The system is developed using the
Agile methodology to support iterative development and continuous user feedback. It
is implemented using the MERN stack, comprising MongoDB, Express.js, Node.js,
and React.js, to ensure a scalable, secure, and user-friendly web application. The
information used in the system analysis was collected using interviews, observations,
and reviewing documents to make sure that the system is used to meet actual
operational requirements. System design is based on the layered architecture and is
implemented with the help of the modern web technologies so that it could be usable,
secure and scaled. Role-based access control is also included to control sensitive
information and provide a user with access to functions corresponding to his/her
duties. Through the substitution of manual operations with a centralized digital one,
the proposed system will decrease the paperwork, reduce the number of errors,
increase the accuracy of data, and improve the service delivery. The project can also
offer a viable and scalable solution that would facilitate future growth and help to
make schools management more efficient.

Chapter One
Introduction
1.1 Background of the Project and the Organization
Haramaya University Non-Boarding Secondary School is a government secondary
school that was formed by the management of Haramaya University. The school is in
the university within the Maya City in the Oromia Regional State in Ethiopia. It was
established some six years ago as part of a national program to contemplate the
Decline of the quality of the secondary education in the country. In this initiative, the
public universities were supposed to have model secondary schools to improve the
standard of education and equip the students with national exams and higher
education standards. The school offers secondary education to students in Grade 9-
Grade 12. All are currently running with 1 section with overall number of students is
more than 158 students. The school has shown good results in academics since its
establishment. The Grade 12 students have taken the national secondary school
leaving exams consecutively over two years and all students have passed them
successfully. This performance indicates the goals of the school being a model school
that fosters the ideal academic performance and preparedness to join the universities.
Haramaya University Non-Boarding Secondary School is administratively run by a
director and a vice director. The management of the school and homeroom teachers
are the ones who mostly work with student academic and administrative records.
Currently, the school has a complete manual system of dealing with student
information. All the information pertaining to students, such as registration records,
attendance, academic performance, disciplinary performances, and promotion, are
kept in hardcopy and kept in the office of the director. The computers present in the
school are only used in doing simple things like transcript preparation, reports, and
official letters through simple office programs. Such documents are kept in hard copy.
The school also enjoys the advantage of having constant access to electricity and the
internet because it is situated in the university campus, but due to the lack of an
integrated digital system, the school has had multiple operational problems. The
problems associated with manual record management are that it exposes data to loss

1.2 Statement of the Problem

all the information of the students. As a result, student registration and the preparation

kept in the director’s office and have to be searched and updated manually. Student
files are sometimes mixed up or hard to find, especially at the time of registration, and

computer and then storing them as hard copies is the only way to solve the problem.
This method of working increases the administrative workload of the school
management, hampers timely service delivery, and causes complaints from students
who find it difficult to get transcripts, reports, and other official documents. In
addition, the lack of a centralized digital system hinders the implementation of the

increase in the number of students (Mr. Ahmed Dado (Asst. Prof), personal

The major problems identified in the existing system include:
 Inefficiency and delays in student registration and report preparation due to
manual record handling and slow retrieval of information.
 Data inconsistency and risk of loss: resulting from missing records duplicated
information, and repeated manual rewriting of student documents.
 High administrative workload and poor service delivery: which limit
management efficiency and lead to student dissatisfaction.

1.4 Feasibility Study
A feasibility study is an analysis undertaken to evaluate genuineity of a proposed
system being developed and executed in an organization. It evaluates the feasibility of
the project based on operation, technical, economic, behavioral and scheduling issues.
The feasibility study is intended to help in ensuring that the proposed system is
feasible, economical, acceptable to the users and that it can operate within the
available resources and time limits to address the organizational requirements [1].

operations of an organization. It analyses the compatibility of the system with the
current work practices, user competencies as well as company processes and the
readiness of users to embrace the new system [1]. The proposed system is

1.6.4 Software Development Methodology
The methodology of software development employed in this project is the Agile
approach, which is an iterative and incremental technique of delivering software in
mini-cycles and that can enable constant feedback and modification. Agile focuses on
flexibility, integration, and change responsiveness through creating the system in
small workable units instead of one massive release. This enables the development
team to clarify requirements, do early testing and incorporate user feedback in the
process to enhance both the quality and relevancy of the end product [3].
In this project, We Will be Implementing Agile SDLC to organize the development in
the iterative parts with each of the parts being dedicated to particular features, like
student registration, attendance management, and reporting modules. The team will
discuss the progress after every iteration, test features, and modify future plans,
depending on the information they receive about stakeholders. This will make sure
that the system conforms to arising needs and functional modules are delivered in
pieces and tested all along.

Chapter Two
Description of the Existing System
2.1 Overview of the Existing System
In HU Non-Boarding School, the majority of the academic and administrative
functions are performed manually with the use of paper-based records. Important
processes like student registration, student attendance, recording of grades, schedule
control, communication with parents and generation of report are done by use of
printed forms, classroom registers, notice boards and spreadsheet saved on the
locations. Though these ways enable the school to operate, they are time-consuming,
prone to errors and cannot enable one to quickly access, verify or analyze information
easily. Components analysis of the existing practices and related issues is as follows:
1. Student Registration and Enrollment: Registration forms are done on printed
forms that are submitted in the admissions office by the parents or guardians. The
student’s identifiers are done manually with an inconsistency in the results, without
all the information provided and sometimes a duplicate record is taken.
Challenge: Students records cannot be easily accessed or updated easily due to the
absence of a central repository, thus this will add more workload to the administration.

2.5 Bottlenecks of the Existing System (Using PIECES Framework)
2.5.1 Performance (Response Time)
The current manual system is time-consuming to handle student data, attendance
records, examination scores in addition to report preparation. Every activity, including
the creation of grades or class schedules, takes a lot of manual effort and time. The
use of physical forms and manual calculation slows down the administration
processes and makes the system less responsive in general.
2.5.2 Input (Inaccurate/Redundant/Flexible) and Output (Inaccurate)
The current system contains data input errors because the student records, attendance,
and grades are manually entered. There is a tendency to have redundant information
that includes several copies of registration forms or several entries of the same grade,
which enhances the potential of inaccuracies. Report cards and performance
summaries which are the output of this may be inaccurate due to errors in
transcription and delays in consolidating the data. Also, the system is not flexible
enough to accommodate any change in the student information or the schedule of
classes.
2.5.3 Security and Controls
The manual system offers a low level of security and control over the student and
academic records. Hard copies can be lost or damaged or accessed by unscrupulous
people. Automated audit trails and access controls have not been implemented and it
is hard to verify the records on its accuracy and integrity. Grading, attendance and
reporting mistakes can escape the system until they are checked manually and this
minimizes the overall control and accountability in the system.
2.6 Forms and other documents of the existing systems
The current system in Haramaya University Non-Boarding Secondary School is quite
intensive as all the student information is managed using paper-based forms and
documents. The types are student registration forms, attendance sheets, academic
report forms and transcripts. The forms are standardized, that is, the same structure is
used in all grades this makes it consistent in recording and reporting of the data. All
the processed forms are held in the physical form in the director office, in the filing
cabinets. The administrators or teachers have to handwrite the forms again and place

manually find the needed documents.

4.2.2 Process (Controller) Layer
Controller or Process Layer controls the flow of control in a system, accepting input
from users, ordering related work to be done and coordinating communication
between other layers of the system. This tier accepts incoming requests, validates
them superficially and figures out what actions should be taken according to the
system rules and user permissions. At the most fundamental level it enforces
application workflows, takes care of authentication and authorization, and ensures
that requests are handled in a coherent way as well as being secure.By separating
control logic from presentation and business logic, the controller layer improves
system modularity and simplifies maintenance[11]
In the case of the Web Based Student Information Management System for HU Non-
Boarding High School, the Controller or Process Layer is implemented with the help
of Node.js and Express.js which is part of the popular MERN stack. Coming from the
user interface, this layer receives RESTful API requests, steers the back-and-forth of
information between the business side and the rest of the system. It’s also in charge of
managing user identities using a combination of username and password and laying
down rules so that Admins, Teachers, Students and Parents can only access what
they’re supposed to. The controller layer is also responsible for sorting out where
requests go, checking the validity of the data coming in, handling sessions or tokens
and bailing out of errors.

application. It encapsulates the main logic, rules and operations that make up the

standpoint of roles, this layer makes sure that not much can be altered in a student’s
academic record unless the person doing it is authorized to do so. The business layer
sends its requests to the controller layer and relies on the persistence layer for data
storage. It doesn't concern itself with how the data is shown or stored.
4.2.4 Persistent Layer
This layer is responsible for managing data storage and retrieval operations in a
system. Coming between the business logic and the database, it conceals the
underlying database specifics, allowing for the seamless storage, updating and
retrieval of data, without exposing this information to other layers. In a layered
system, it’s precisely this Persistence Layer that ensures the consistency, integrity and
dependability of the data, and by isolating the persistence concerns from the business
logic, also reduces the ‘tightly coupled’ design and gives systems a much more
manageable, scalable and flexible nature. [11]
In Web Based Student Information Management System for HU Non-Boarding High
School, the Persistence Layer is supported by using MongoDB as part of the MERN
stack. This layer is responsible for storing, retrieving and manipulating system
data of students, teachers, parents, attendance information marks on the test timetable

===== PAGE 46 =====
Page | 35
and user account details etc. Mongoose represents a available Object Data Modeling
(ODM) library that is designed to work with data: defining the structure of the schema,
handle relationships between these schemas and is used to translate objects in code to
their representation back there where your application stores its data (in most cases an
MongoDB database). The persistence layer provides a mechanism to carry out data
operations that are requested by the business layer in an effective way, while
preserving the integrity of the data and assisting performance. Static methods don't
allow for easy extendibility or purging of the database.
4.3 Class Modeling
Class Modeling is an insinuation of its static aspect finding its top-level classes, their
attributes, operations and relations. In UML, a class diagram is a visual representation
of system entities and their interrelations that indicate how data and responsibilities
are decomposed in the system domain in which they interact. Attributes specify the
properties of each class, operations represent the behaviors associated with classes
and relationships like association, aggregation, composition and inheritance explain
how classes are linked to one another. Class modeling is the pattern of system
implementation, which ensures consistency between system requirements and design,
enhances clarity, maintainability and prospects for future extension of the system [11]
for suggested Web Based Student Information Management System for HU Non-
Boarding High School.

4.4 State Chart Modeling
State chart modeling is used to express the dynamic behavior of a system by
illustrating how an object changes state in response to events over time. A state chart
diagram in UML explains the various states of an object and the transitions between
those states, as well as the events that cause these transitions [11]. Such modeling is
particularly employed in systems in which entities go through a lifecycle, e.g., user
accounts, student records, or academic processes. State chart diagrams, by drawing
pictorial depictions of state transitions, support visualization of system behavior,
validation of logic and diagnosis of potential errors in system operation.
In Web Based Student Information Management System for HU Non-Boarding High
School state chart modeling is adopted to describe how important system entities shift
from one state to another side periodically during normal operation of the system,
increasing clarity and facilitating accurate systems design.

In case of the Web Based Student Information Management System the testing is
directed to test the main functionality of the system like authentication and user
management, student information, attendance, academic records, timetable, reporting,

ensured that all the parts and the entire system are functioning well in the conditions
as expected.

5.2.1 Unit Testing
Unit testing involves testing part or modules of a system/ system to make sure that
every component works as it should. Unit testing in the Web Based Student
Information Management System for HU non Boarding School will be used in core
modules in the system which include user login, user management, student record
processing, attendance management, academic record processing, timetable

5.4 User manual preparation
This section explains how various users will use the Web Based SIMS for the HU
non-Boarding School by a step wise account of interaction with the system. This
system is developed with role-based access in place of which every user can only
interrelate with functions that are pertinent to his or her duties.
Administrator Usage
The administrator can log in by first accessing the login page where he/she enters a
valid username and password to use the system. When an administrator successfully
logs in, he or she is redirected to the admin dashboard where he or she sees system
overview information. The administrator can create and manage user accounts of
teachers, students and parents, preset roles, permissions and changes system settings,
all from the dashboard. With its help, the administrator will be able to enroll new
students, maintain records of students, schedule classes, and compile such reports as
attendance summaries and academic performance reports. The administrator is also
able to check the system messages and also to send messages to other users through
the messaging option before leaving the system.
Teacher Usage
A teacher gets into the system with the help of credentials assigned. On logging in,
the teacher dashboard appears, which includes assigned classes, and subjects. The
teacher is able to keep track of daily attendance by checking a class and marking
students present or absent. The teacher is also able to add and amend academic
records of students, see the school timetables and create academic or attendance
records to classes assigned. Moreover, the teacher is able to send and receive
messages to and with the administrators, students and parents through the messaging
module. Once the tasks are done, the teacher logs out safely.

Student Usage
To use the system, a student logs on to the system with his or her username and
password. Upon successful authentication, the student dashboard appears, and it lists
personal and academic information. The student is able to access his or her profile

of course, using the dashboard. The student is also able to read messages posted by

and hence they are easy and secure. The typical actions of the system that include
login, logout, reading information and sending a message have standardized
interaction patterns to ensure that there is less confusion to the user and the system
becomes easier to use.

Chapter Six
Conclusion and Recommendation
6.1 Conclusion
The proposed Web-Based Student Information Management System (SIMS) of
Haramaya University Non-Boarding Secondary School will deal with the issues that
are found in the current manual system. The school is currently using paper-based
processes to register students, keep academic records, manage attendance, prepare
reports and communicated and this is the reason why the school loses data, slows
down and the administration is overworked.
A web-based solution, planned through careful system analysis, modeling and design,
will do:
 Manage student records digitally, in a centralized and organized form.
 Automate major functions of the institution including registration, attendance,
updating of academic records, and generating of reports.
 Offer role-based dashboards to administrators, teachers, students and parents so
that only the relevant information can be accessed by the user.
 Integrate a messaging platform to be used in communication.
The documentation and design proves that a web-based system could contribute to
efficiency, error reduction, as well as access to information in time, be mobile-
responsive, and scalable to use in the future.
All in all, the project offers an analyzed, feasible, and applicable solution to
modernize the information management of students in the school.
